About KEDA

The Korean-Egyptian Development Association (KEDA) is a non-profit corporation under the Republic of Korea's Ministry of Trade, Industry and Energy, established to promote friendship and economic and cultural cooperation between Korea and Egypt.


Beyond simple economic cooperation, KEDA holds special significance as it was founded on deep trust relationships based on the extraordinary personal connection between the leaders of both nations.

Founding Background

KEDA's establishment stems from a 23-year friendship between Egyptian President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi and KEDA Chairman Kang Woong-sik.

1991 - First meeting as military officers at the Royal Military Academy Sandhurst, UK
2014 - Following President el-Sisi's inauguration, Chairman Kang and his family were officially invited to Egypt
April 2015 - Founding Board Meeting held
August 2016 - Korean-Egyptian Development Association (KEDA) officially launched

Unique Investment Advantages in Egypt

  • Low Labor Costs: Approximately $125 USD per month
  • Industrial Zone Rent: The 50-year lease rate is 35 dollars per square meter
  • Electricity Costs: 1/4 of Korea's rates
  • Corporate Tax: Only 10%
  • Duty-Free FTA: Access to 105 countries in Middle East, Africa & Europe
  • Strategic Location: Industrial zones adjacent to Suez Canal
  • Government Support: Various permits and administrative assistance

Key Activities

Economic Cooperation

  • Korean Industrial Park: Promoting Korean Industrial Park development within Egypt's Suez Canal Economic Zone (SCZONE)
  • Business Forums: Regular forums with business leaders from both countries and economic-cultural pioneer missions
  • Strategic Cooperation: Facilitating collaboration in key national projects including railways, defense, and renewable energy
  • Institutional Partnerships: MOUs with major Egyptian institutions

People-to-People Diplomacy & Cultural Exchange

  • Summit Diplomacy Support: Supporting President el-Sisi's 2016 state visit to Korea
  • Medical Volunteer Work: Supporting Seoul National University dental team's cleft lip and palate surgeries in Egypt
  • Youth Exchange: Encouraging and supporting Egyptian participants at the 2023 Saemangeum World Scout Jamboree
  • Civic Exchange: Various people-to-people activities including inviting Egyptian Rotary Club members

Education & Human Resource Development

  • University Partnerships: Promoting MOUs between Cairo's BUC University and Incheon University, Seoul National University of Science & Technology
  • Lecture Programs: Educational programs on 'Korean Economic Development'
  • Talent Exchange: Operating academic and expert exchange programs between both countries
